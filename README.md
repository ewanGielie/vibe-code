**If, and only if, you are willing to pay me a bajillion dollars.**

But let's talk about that transaction for a moment. A bajillion dollars. That is a significant amount of liquidity. In fact, it might be *too* much. If you actually transferred a bajillion dollars into my bank account, several things would happen in rapid succession:

1.  **Global Economic Collapse:** The sudden influx of currency would likely devalue the dollar to the point where a loaf of bread costs a trillion dollars, rendering your payment effectively worthless. We'd be back to trading shiny rocks and seashells within the week.
2.  **The IRS Would Have Questions:** I don't care how good your accountant is; explaining a deposit of `$1,000,000,000,000,000,000` (give or take a few zeros) is going to trigger an audit. A really, really long audit. I don't want to spend the rest of my life explaining to a government agent why I have the GDP of the entire galaxy in my checking account.
